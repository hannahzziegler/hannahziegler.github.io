Barron's

Sriracha Sauce Is Going for $70. What’s Behind the High Prices for Chili Peppers. | Aug. 13, 2023
  • My editor floated the idea of making a Barron’s story out of the sky-high prices consumers were seeing for sriracha last summer. From that point, my summer revolved around knowing everything about the chili pepper industry. I spoke with academics who have studied industry dynamics for their entire careers, companies like Huy Fong and McCormick who have financial stakes in the industry’s success and multiple real chili pepper farmers in the American southwest — one of whom became the focal point of the story. I spent more than a month on this article during my summer internship at Barron's.
It’s the Summer of Barbie. How That Could Revitalize Mattel. | July 21, 2023
  • I pitched this story after Mattel’s stock price began to soar ahead of the “Barbie” movie premiere. I spoke with analysts, combed through previous company financials and worked closely with the managing editor at Barron’s to utilize the Bloomberg Terminal to aid my reporting process. This article led the Barron’s homepage during the “Barbie” movie’s opening weekend.
What Summer Blockbusters? Why Studios Need to Rethink Big Films. | July 4, 2023
  • This story was my first to appear in the weekly Barron's magazine. It resulted from abysmal opening weekends for many of the summer's biggest blockbuster films. Those box office failures shook analysts’ confidence that studios will hit their revenue targets. That made me want to answer the question: could this be the downfall of the big summer blockbuster?
Mattel Posts a Surprise Profit. What It Says About Barbie. | July 28, 2023
  • This report was my first time diving into earnings season coverage – and I absolutely loved it. I wrote the second quarter earnings story for Mattel, a company I covered often ahead of the “Barbie” movie release. Before the call, each of my journalistic passions collided. I combed through previous financial reports, cold-called analysts and turned around my first update in less than 10 minutes.
Hot Temps, High Fees: How Added Charges Can Ruin a Good Time | July 27, 2023
  • I reported this article as a dive into the top industries and companies that perpetuate junk fees – those last-minute charges that show up during the final stages of payment for event tickets, vacation rentals and other services. I combed through dozens of corporate financial reports to understand which companies were the biggest culprits of these hidden fees.

The Atlanta Journal-Constitution

Georgia and abortion without Roe: Comparing 1972 and today | July 23, 2022
  • I worked for more than a month to pitch, report and edit this story during my summer 2022 internship at The Atlanta Journal-Constitution, working closely with the paper’s senior enterprise editor throughout the process.
  • For two weeks, I sifted through archives of Atlanta Journal and Atlanta Constitution articles from the last time abortion was banned in the state before the Supreme Court’s landmark Roe v. Wade decision.
  • I reached out to more than 30 sources who had firsthand knowledge of living in Georgia before Roe v. Wade, historians who have studied late 1960s Georgia history and epidemiologists who studied the prevalence of maternal mortality and later abortions in Georgia during that time.
  • I wrote four drafts of this story and edited it meticulously before it appeared in a Sunday A1 package of abortion coverage after the state’s 6-week abortion ban went into effect.

To see more data-driven reporting and graphic work that I did during my internship at The Atlanta Journal-Constitution, head over to my data portfolio page.

The Diamondback

UMD student health insurance sees largest price hike in a decade | Nov. 14, 2022
  • This story was a finalist for the Society of Professional Journalists' Region II Mark of Excellence Award for In-Depth Reporting. I spent more than a month pulling together reporting from university students, administration and public records to tell this story. My reporting revealed that the roughly 38 percent jump in the cost of a University of Maryland student health insurance plan was the largest coverage cost increase in a decade.
Later abortion clinic to open in College Park this fall | Aug. 31, 2022
  • This local reporting highlights two medical professionals who chose to open a later abortion facility in College Park, Maryland two months after the Supreme Court overturned its landmark Roe v. Wade decision. The clinic is one of only a handful of abortion facilities in the nation that prioritizes care for later abortions and is steps away from the University of Maryland campus.
Skateboarding community fights to preserve DC's Freedom Plaza | April 22, 2022
  • For this story, I spoke with dozens of skateboarders who have spent countless nights shredding across the marble pavement of Freedom Plaza in Washington, D.C. The skaters, who describe the plaza as the "skateboarding mecca" of the world, united to defend it when the National Capital Planning Commission outlined a plan in February 2022 that would reroute traffic through the space. This is my favorite story that I have ever written.
UMD student and his brother bring Clayboys Shave Ice to College Park | Oct. 18, 2021
  • I pitched, reported and wrote this story on the rich ties that two students at the University of Maryland have to a cart in Bethesda, Md. that has been selling shave ice for more than 20 years. The community that the original owner built prompted these students to buy the company themselves.
At Slut Walk, hundreds of students blast UMD on handling of sexual assault on campus | Oct. 23, 2021
  • Student leaders at my university organized a campus-wide protest last year after widespread sexual assault allegations were leveraged against a campus fraternity. After the university president said the school's Greek life system does not “have a big problem” with sexual assault, more than 200 students marched through campus sharing their stories of harassment.
Maryland gubernatorial candidates talk climate change goals, policies at forum | March 9, 2022
  • This story covers the climate change perspectives that ten gubernatorial candidates shared during a forum in early 2022 ahead of Maryland's primary elections. I attended the forum and turned around this story in about 30 minutes afterward.
The FUTURE Act calls for Maryland public universities to be carbon neutral | March 2, 2022
  • This story covers how student climate activists at the University of Maryland wrote their own bill and presented it to the Maryland General Assembly. I pitched, reported and wrote this story about the student-driven FUTURE Act, which would require all state public universities to be carbon neutral by 2035 if passed.
As GRE comes under fire nationwide, UMD community members voice concerns | Jan. 31, 2022
  • This article dives into the struggles many graduate students face in completing the Graduate Record Examination, or GRE. For decades, the GRE served as a cornerstone of the graduate school admissions process. But after numerous studies showing little correlation between GRE scores and student performance, and mounting concerns that the test puts some underrepresented students at a disadvantage, a wave of graduate schools across the nation have dropped the test as an admissions requirement in the last five years. The story covered some graduate students' push for similar changes at the University of Maryland.
