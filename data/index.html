Data Reporting

The Diamondback

For transfer students, housing problems amplify stressful transition to UMD | Nov. 2, 2021
  • My analysis of university data obtained through two Maryland Public Information Act requests found that less than 5 percent of students in our university's housing system across the last five years have been transfer students. I found students impacted by the issue of housing affordability for late-admit transfers and built the story around the data and their anecdotes.
UMD sees small rise in enrollment for Black freshmen from Prince George’s County | Dec. 14, 2021
    UMD sees lowest admissions rate in over a decade for 2020-2021 cycle | Nov. 17, 2021
    • This story came out of annual university enrollment data that I helped analyze and visualize. My university saw a sharp decrease in its acceptance rate after moving its application to the CommonApp – prompting many students, staff and administrators to weigh in on the future of Maryland's flagship state school's accessibility.

    The Atlanta Journal-Constitution

    Gas prices: the ups and downs, the ins and outs | Aug. 5, 2021
    • I spent a cumulative 40 hours working on graphics and presentation in this enterprise article on gas prices in Georgia. I created each graphic below using a combination of data visualization techniques in Datawrapper, Adobe Illustrator and Photoshop. I brought a consistent visual style to this article that helped each section of the story maintain a visual flow. These graphics and the accompanying story appeared on A1 in The Atlanta Journal-Constitution's Sunday, Aug. 7 edition.

    Staff shortage puts elderly in jeopardy at many Georgia nursing homes | July 7, 2022
    • I helped a fellow AJC intern with data analysis and a quick visualization for her investigative piece on nursing home shortages in Georgia. My analysis in R pulled data from the Centers for Medicare and Medicaid Services, filtered out Georgia and obtained the percentage of nursing homes reporting shortages.
    • Later in the summer, I used this dataset to help another reporter in the newsroom compare COVID rates among nursing home staff and residents. (My visuals are on pages 4, 6 and 7)
